Determination of Kinematic Indeterminacy


Objective:

Determination of Kinematic Indeterminacy of the structures





SOME FACTS:

(1) Kinematic indeterminacy can lead to structural instablity if the additional degrees of freedom are not properly constrained.

(2) Kinematic indeterminacy can be caused by the presence of redundant members in a structure. A redundant member is a member that can be removed from the structure without affecting its stability..

(3) The concept of kinematic indeterminacy is also important in other fields, such as robotics and biomechanics, where it is used to describe the motion of complex mechanisms and joints.
